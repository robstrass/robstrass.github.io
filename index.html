Who is Rob?

I've spent most of my life enjoying the diversity and beauty of the Bay Area. Originally intending to go into Law Enforcement after college, I found the tumultuous nature of the job and differing personal views to be enough to look for another professional calling. After some soul-searching and unfulfilling jobs, I discovered programming during the height of the pandemic.

I attended App Academy in July 2021 and dedicated the next 6 months to becoming a proficient Software Engineer. After graduating in January 2022, I began looking for my first job; I quickly learned that although I knew how to code, that was only half of the battle. After spending countless hours preparing for interviews and continuing my personal development, I landed my first role as a Software Engineer.

It didn't take long for me to realize that nothing could prepare me for the gap between what I knew to what I needed to learn to be a successful developer. With the help of Senior developers and colleagues on my team, I quickly acclimated myself and got busy making a difference within my small company.

While at SPJ Solutions, I went from working on small enhancement and bug fix tickets to taking over an entire feature used by our consultants and Fortune 500 clients in my 3rd month with the company. I had to learn and grow quickly to take over the feature, which was previously developed by 2 of our best engineers before I was handed the reigns.

Along with many other professionals in the tech industry, I recently found myself out of work. I'm proficient in Typescript, React, Redux, Node.js, and PostgreSQL databases, and am currently looking for my next Software Engineering role!



Skills

I'm proficient in programming in TypeScript, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and also have a familiarity with Python and SQL. While in my last role, I worked with React, Redux thunks and sagas, Express, Node.js, and many smaller libraries, including Bluebird and Axios for asynchronous functionality.

Since my last role revolved around VMware's NSX network virtualization platform, I became well versed in working with third party API's and reading documentation for those API's. Being the only engineer working on the feature I was assigned to, I became very familiar with certain parts of the NSX API that no other engineers on my team had interacted with or were familiar with.

In addition to the technical skills outlined above, I learned quite a few soft skills during my time in my last role at SPJ Solutions, and at App Academy while learning programming fundamentals. SPJ Solutions followed Agile software development principles, including the use of JIRA for task management and monthly sprints. I also actively participated in daily team standups to discuss blockers and current tasks, along with regular code reviews. I also worked in pair programming settings in my last role and while attending App Academy.
